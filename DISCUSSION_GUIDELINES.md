# Noble Beacon — Discussion Guidelines

> "A title is a lantern, not a crown. Leaders illuminate the path — they do not own it."

Welcome to the Noble Beacon community discussions. This space operates under the same principles as the archive itself: **sovereignty, privacy, and stewardship before ownership.**

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Zero Extraction
- No data mining. No engagement farming. No algorithmic manipulation.
- Your words belong to you. GitHub's platform hosts them, but Noble Beacon will never monetize, analyze, or sell community discourse.

### 2. Sovereign Participation
- No accounts beyond your GitHub identity are required.
- You are not a "user." You are a contributor, a witness, a fellow traveler.
- Pseudonymity is respected. Real names are never required.

### 3. Creative Encouragement
- The most outlandish and creative ideas are actively encouraged.
- There is no such thing as a "stupid question" in this space.
- Speculation, imagination, and philosophical tangents are welcome — they are how new volumes are born.

### 4. WebWarden Anti-Bullying Covenant
- **Strong safeguards against bullying** are enforced.
- Disagreement is healthy. Disrespect is not.
- Ad hominem attacks, harassment, doxxing, or intimidation result in immediate removal.
- If you witness harmful behavior, flag it. The community protects itself.

### 5. Thread Revival ("Necro") Is Permitted
- No knowledge is wasted. Old threads may contain seeds that only germinate years later.
- Reviving a dormant discussion with new insight is encouraged, not punished.
- The archive does not forget. Neither should we.

## Privacy Provisions

### What We Collect
- **Nothing.** Noble Beacon does not operate analytics, tracking, or monitoring on GitHub Discussions.

### What GitHub Collects
- GitHub's own privacy policy applies to their platform. We cannot control this.
- We recommend reviewing [GitHub's Privacy Statement](https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/privacy-policies/github-general-privacy-statement).

### What We Recommend
- Use a pseudonym if privacy is important to you.
- Do not share personal information (real name, location, employer) unless you choose to.
- Never share another person's information without their explicit consent.

## The Warding Principle in Practice

> "Every beauty has a shadow. Name it."

When proposing ideas or features, we encourage you to also name the potential harms:
- Who could this exclude?
- What power does this concentrate?
- How could this be misused?

This is not pessimism — it is the Ward-Keeper's gift. Naming shadows prevents them from growing unseen.

---

## Moderation Philosophy

- **Light touch, strong boundaries.** We do not over-moderate. We do not police tone. We do protect people.
- **Transparency.** If a post is removed, the reason is stated publicly (without quoting the removed content).
- **No hidden power.** Moderators are named. Their actions are visible. Their authority is borrowed, not owned.
- **Repair over punishment.** First response to a violation is always a private message explaining the concern. Removal is a last resort.

---

## How to Participate

1. **Introduce yourself** in General — which path resonates with you?
2. **Ask questions** in Q&A — no question is too basic or too philosophical.
3. **Share ideas** in Ideas — what should the next volume contain?
4. **Show your work** in Show and Tell — glyphs, games, interpretations, music, anything.
5. **Vote** in Polls — help shape the direction of the archive.

---

## The Oath

By participating in Noble Beacon discussions, you affirm:

- I will not extract value from others without their consent.
- I will name hidden power when I see it.
- I will encourage creative risk-taking in others.
- I will steward this space as if future generations will read it.
